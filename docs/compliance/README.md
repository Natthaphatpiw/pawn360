# Astly - PDPA Compliance Document Pack

Status: Living document set, prepared for investor technical due diligence and operational adoption.
Owner: Data Protection Officer (DPO) [to be appointed], Astly Co., Ltd. [registration to confirm].

This folder breaks the analytical parent document [`../../DATA_PRIVACY_COMPLIANCE.md`](../../DATA_PRIVACY_COMPLIANCE.md)
into the **individual, adoptable policy documents** that a due-diligence reviewer typically requests.
Each file is written to serve two purposes at once: a data-room artifact for investors, and a real
policy Astly can put into force.

> Legal note: These documents map Astly's implementation to Thailand's Personal Data Protection Act
> (PDPA, B.E. 2562 / 2019) based on the official translation and reputable practitioner sources. They are
> compliance-planning input, **not legal advice**; every conclusion must be validated by qualified Thai
> data-protection counsel before reliance.

## How the pack fits together

- The **Privacy Policy** is the outward-facing notice (PDPA Sec 23) and points to the operational
  policies below for detail.
- The **RoPA** (Sec 39) is the master internal register of every processing activity and feeds the
  Privacy Policy, Consent Policy, and Processor Agreements.
- **Consent** (Sec 19 / Sec 26) governs the explicit biometric-eKYC consent and any marketing opt-in.
- **Retention & Deletion** (Sec 37(3) / Sec 33) defines how long each data category is kept, reconciled
  with the AML 5-year obligation.
- **Data-Breach Notification** (Sec 37(4)) is the 72-hour incident runbook.
- **Processor Agreements** (Sec 40) is the processor register, the mandatory DPA clauses, and the
  execution tracker.
